Swimming Technique

Freestyle(Front Crawl) Technique

Swimmers utilize an alternate arm storke mixed with flutter kicks to breathe on the side during freestyle(front crawl).

Backstroke Technique

Swimmers are on their backs,amploying an alternating arm stroke with a flutter kick, focusing on head posture to ensure they do not exceed 90 degress of rotation.

Breaststroke Technique

Involves a simultaneous arm pull and frog kick, with both hands contacting at the same time during the glide phase and demands precise breathing timing.

Butterfly Stroke Technique

A simultaneous arm pull with an undulating dolphin kick in which bot arms and must move simultaneously, and swimmers must keep their arms above the water.

Turns and Streamlines Technique

Properly executed turns involve touching the wall with both hands and executing an efficient push-off streamline position underwater to maximize speed.

Breathing Technique

Coordinating breathing with stroke technique is crucial. In most strokes, swimmers aim to breathe without disrupting their rhythm by timings breaths with their stroke cycle.
